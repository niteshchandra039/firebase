About Me

Hi, I'm Nitesh Kumar, an Assistant Professor in Astronomy and Astrophysics at UPES, Dehradun. I have a Ph.D. from the University of Delhi, where I worked under Prof. H. P. Singh. My research focuses on applying Machine Learning and AI-based algorithms to analyze stellar photometric and spectroscopic data.

Research Interests

  • Automated analysis of astronomical data
  • Machine Learning for photometric & spectroscopic surveys
  • High-resolution spectroscopy and chemical abundance analysis
  • Stellar variability and distance indicators in the Milky Way
  • LSST data analysis and transient surveys

Current Work

At UPES, I'm actively setting up a small observatory, equipping it with a telescope for research and student training. I also teach courses on Computational Astrophysics, Data Analysis, and Quantum Mechanics.

Teaching

I teach courses on Computational Astrophysics, Data Analysis, and Quantum Mechanics at UPES. I'm also involved in supervising undergraduate and postgraduate projects in Astronomy.
